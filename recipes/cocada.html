Cocada

Description

Cocada is a popular street food sold everywhere in Brazil. It's a sweet made with grated fresh coconut. There are many variations, you can try adding any flavoring that you like!.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 and 1/2 cups white sugar
  • 2 and 1/2 cups grated fresh coconut, about 500 grams

Steps

  1. In a saucepan, add water and sugar, mix lightly. Heat over medium heat, without stirring, until the sugar syrup forms a thread when lifted and dropped by a spoon, about 20 minutes. Brush the sides of the pan with water if any sugar crystals are formed.
  2. Add grated coconut, mix just enough to coat every thread of coconut with sugar syrup. Cook without stirring for 5 minutes.
  3. Transfer the cocada with the syrup to a bowl, let rest for 5 minutes. Grease a sheet pan with butter or line with parchment paper.
  4. Using spoons, shape the cocadas while hot and humid as you like. You can grab some amount with the spoon and drop it like a mound, shape it using the spoons like a quenelle, etc.
  5. Let the cocadas dry out at room temperature, for about one hour. They form a crunchy shell on the outside and are still tender inside.
